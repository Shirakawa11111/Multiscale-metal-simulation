## 1. 总体定位

This project has assembled a clean, internally-consistent PFC mechanism corpus, but its scientific weight is uneven: roughly 70% of the verified conclusions are confirmatory re-demonstrations of established PFC/MD physics (glide, dipole annihilation, multiplication/avalanche, void emission, GB stick-slip, BCC Bain-instability), and the genuinely additive elements are narrow. Versus the field, the work sits squarely inside the Elder-Grant / Chan-Goldenfeld / Berry / Skaugen-Skogvoll / Trautt-Adland lineage and does not advance the method frontier — it does not implement the elastic/plastic timescale separation that the 2014-2022 corrective literature (Heinonen, Skaugen, Skogvoll) made standard, which is the single largest exposure for every dynamics-dependent claim. Its two real, defensible contributions are (a) a systematic, statistically-robust strain-rate-sensitivity ladder m(r) swept toward the liquid spinodal with non-overlapping bootstrap CIs (0.19->0.45), which has no clean PFC precedent, and (b) a documented, density-matched HDF5 coupling contract from a DAMASK crystal-plasticity ROI into a PFC box, which fills a genuine integration gap. The strongest publishable paper is a focused, honestly-scoped methods+phenomenology piece built on the m(r) sweep plus the cross-mode GB test and the ROI-matched mechanism demonstration — NOT the broad "we built Layer 4b and reproduced everything" framing the current outline implies. The biggest risk across the board is overclaiming novelty and quoting absolute physical numbers (m=0.45 as superplastic, 10-12% as a material BCC limit, "experimentally-relevant density") that the conserved-diffusive dynamics cannot support without an elastically-relaxed cross-check.

### Grain boundaries as dislocation sources vs. sliding/shear-coupled migration: low
*现状*：The modern consensus is that grain-boundary-mediated plasticity (sliding, shear-coupled migration, grain rotation, stress-assisted growth) is carried by DISCONNECTIONS — line defects with combined step + Burgers-vector character — whose step/dislocation amplitude ratio selects whether the GB slides, couples, or migrates (Rajabzadeh, Mompiou, Legros & Combe, PRL 110:265507, 2013; "almost unifying theory" reviews). Cahn, Mishin & Suzuki (Acta Mater 54:4953, 2006) established the geometric coupling factor beta: low-angle and special boundaries are strongly shear-COUPLED, beta is multivalued and switches branch near ~35deg tilt, and high-angle GBs cross over from coupling to pure SLIDING as temperature rises. Stress-driven GB shear is intrinsically STICK-SLIP: a sawtooth stress with drops that coincide with discrete GB-position jumps (disconnection nucleation/glide), with drop magnitude decreasing as system size grows (Mishin, Suzuki, Uberuaga & Voter, PRB 75:224101, 2007). Emission of LATTICE dislocations from a clean, low-energy boundary is a high-stress event near the ideal strength: the Sigma-5 (310) tilt GB in Cu emits (partial) dislocations only at ~6.28 GPa tensile / ~3.08 GPa resolved (Spearot, Tschopp, McDowell & Tucker; Sci. Rep. 4:5919, 2014; IJP 2007), and emission is a structural-unit/boundary-source process, NOT an intragranular Frank-Read loop. General/high-energy boundaries and those with E structural units, ledges, free volume, or solute complexions emit far more readily than coincident special boundaries; in nanocrystals emission accommodates GB sliding only once local stresses are very high (Van Swygenhoven; Tucker & McDowell; Pan & Sansoz). Twist boundaries specifically carry screw-dislocation networks that can glide in-plane, so they tend to slide/shear rather than throw loops into the grains. Crucially, PFC and amplitude-PFC ALREADY reproduce shear-coupled GB migration AND the coupling-to-sliding transition with coupling factors in quantitative agreement with CMS theory and MD (Trautt, Adland, Karma & Mishin, Acta Mater 60:6528, 2012; Adland, Karma, Spatschek et al., PRB 87:024110, 2013, bcc Fe).

### Strain-rate sensitivity exponent m and the glide -> GB-sliding/superplasticity -
*现状*：The canonical m-value taxonomy is well established and matches our project's conceptual mapping: dislocation glide in coarse-grained metals gives low m ~ 0.01-0.05 (stress exponent n~4-10); grain-boundary sliding (GBS) accommodated superplasticity gives m ~ 0.3-0.5 (n~2, grain-size exponent p~2, activation energy of GB diffusion); and Nabarro-Herring / Coble diffusional (Newtonian-viscous) creep gives m ~ 1 (n=1). m is empirically known to RISE with increasing temperature (homologous T) and with DECREASING grain size into the nc/UFG regime, where GB-mediated processes (GB diffusion + sliding + GB-source dislocation emission) dominate; nc-fcc metals show ~10x enhancement of m over coarse-grained counterparts, and m can approach ~0.8-1 for the finest grains. Superplastic flow shows a sigmoidal log(sigma)-log(rate) curve with a peak-m "Region II". However, standard (Elder-Grant) PFC uses purely diffusive, overdamped conserved dynamics with NO inertia and NO separation between elastic-wave and diffusion timescales; its "strain rate" lives in arbitrary diffusive-time units, so absolute rates are not directly physical.

### PFC method variants for realistic metals: the 2D-triangular / one-mode-BCC vs FC
*现状*：It is a textbook, universally-acknowledged result that the standard one-mode Elder-Grant PFC stabilizes only the 2D triangular and 3D BCC lattices (the RLVs that form closed "triads"); stripes/rods and the liquid are the only coexisting phases, and the elastic-modulus ratio is not freely tunable. Making PFC describe FCC (and thus Cu/Al) is a known, well-trodden methodological path with two mature branches: (i) multi-mode PFC, where Wu, Adland & Karma (2010, Phys. Rev. E 81, 061601) showed a two-mode coupling of <111> and <200> density waves is REQUIRED to stabilize a mechanically sound FCC with non-zero tetragonal shear modulus; and (ii) the structural/XPFC family of Greenwood, Provatas & Rottler (2010, PRL 105, 045702) and Greenwood, Ofori-Opoku, Rottler & Provatas (2011, Phys. Rev. B 84, 064104), which engineers the two-point direct correlation function to select BCC/FCC/HCP/SC and tune surface/elastic energies. For larger scales, the amplitude-expansion APFC (origin Goldenfeld, Athreya, Dantzig 2005; binary by Elder/Huang/Provatas; reviewed by Salvalaglio & Elder 2022, MSMSE 30, 053001) coarse-grains PFC into slowly-varying complex amplitudes, supports FCC via a seven-amplitude two-mode set, and accesses micrometer scales with adaptive meshing. Dislocations appear naturally as composite topological defects (zeros/phase windings of the amplitudes / Burgers vectors); the modern detection machinery is the lattice distortion / dislocation-density tensor and "lattice incompatibility field" of Skaugen, Angheluta & Viñals (2018) and Skogvoll, Skaugen, Angheluta, Salvalaglio & Viñals (2021-2022), which also handle 3D BCC loops. Plasticity/deformation in PFC is itself established: Stefanovic, Haataja & Provatas (2009, Phys. Rev. E 80, 046107, the MPFC method) did nanocrystalline tension showing elastic-then-glide yield (~1% strain) and grain-size effects; Chan, Goldenfeld & Dantzig and later Skaugen/Salvalaglio mapped PFC plasticity onto the mean-field depinning universality class; Berry, Rottler, Sinclair & Provatas (2015, Phys. Rev. B 92, 134101 / arXiv:1509.02565) did constant-stress creep and extracted strain-rate/grain-size exponents; and PFC polycrystals are repeatedly shown to give inverse Hall-Petch via GB-sliding + GB-diffusion creep. Dislocation nucleation in PFC is governed by a Schmid-like critical resolved-stress criterion (Skogvoll et al. 2021, Phys. Rev. B 103, 014107), and void/precipitate dislocation emission, Frank-Read sources, and the Gibbs-Thomson effect have all been reproduced (e.g. the PFC review of Berry/Provatas/Rottler/Sinclair, arXiv:1403.0601).
